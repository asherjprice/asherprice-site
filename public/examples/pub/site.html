Our Story

A hundred-odd years and counting

The Colliers Arms has stood at the heart of Pontypool since 1887, built to serve the men coming off shift at the ironworks and the coal levels that gave us our name. Four generations of landlords have kept the fires lit and the taps flowing.


We've had our share of history — survived two wars, the miners' strike, and that time Dai Evans tried to bring a sheep in on New Year's Eve. Through it all, we've stayed the same: a proper valleys pub where everyone's welcome, the beer's well-kept, and the craic is free.


These days we pair that tradition with a love of Welsh craft ale. Our six rotating taps showcase the best from breweries across South Wales and beyond. But don't worry — there's always a Brains on for the traditionalists.
